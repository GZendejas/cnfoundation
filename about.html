About

Carrie Nakamura

Founder

Carrie grew up in San Jose, CA. Throughout her childhood, her parents always stressed the importance of volunteer work, and giving back to her community.

After a successful and very lucrative career in venture capital, Carrie was able to retire at an early age and dedicate her life to her true passion - helping people. She founded the CARE foundation in 2015, and through CARE's programs, has provided services and funds for needy people in the Bay Area and abroad.

Esther Okafor

International Operations

Esther grew up in a poor neighborhood in Lagos, Nigeria. She was able to go to primary school through a local NGO. Eventually, she continued her studies at University of Lagos, and later earned a PhD in education from Stanford. Esther worked for several international aid organizations in Africa and Asia. The focus of her work has been providing access to schooling for poverty-stricken children abroad.

She began working with CARE in 2016, and oversees fundraising for causes around the world.

Sal Lopez

Local Operations

Sal is from Chicago, Il. He movied to the Bay Area to attend UC Berkeley, were he earned a bachelor's degree in sociology. He worked for several community organizations in Oakland and the Central Valley.

He joined CARE in 2015, organizing the foundation's first few after-school programs for low income children in the Bay Area. Most recently, he's implemented CARE's housing and homeless initiatives in San Jose. He oversees CARE's local Bay Area operations.
